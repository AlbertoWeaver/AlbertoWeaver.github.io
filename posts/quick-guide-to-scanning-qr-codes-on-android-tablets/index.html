Q: What is a QR code?

A: A QR code (or Quick Response code) is a type of matrix barcode that can store various types of information, including links to websites or apps, contact information, calendar events, and even files. It is used in many industries including retail and marketing to quickly and easily provide detailed information and can be scanned with any modern smartphone or tablet.

Q: How do I scan a QR code with my Android tablet? A: To scan a QR code with your Android tablet, open the camera app and point the device at the QR code. When the camera recognizes the code, a notification will appear on the tablet offering you the option to open it. Tap the notification to open it and view the content.

Q: Can I scan QR codes with the Google Lens app? A: Yes, you can use the Google Lens app on your Android tablet to scan QR codes. To use the app, open the Google Lens app and point it at the QR code. When the code is recognized, a notification will appear offering the option to open it.

Q: What is the difference between a QR code and a barcode? A: The main difference between a barcode and a QR code is the amount of information they can store. Barcodes are limited to storing only numeric information, while QR codes are capable of storing a much larger variety of data, including web links, contact information, audio, and even pictures.

Q: Are QR codes secure? A: Generally, QR codes are considered to be secure. However, as with any technology, it is important to take precautions to ensure the security of your data. Make sure you only scan codes from trusted sources and you should be safe.
