First, make sure you have the right tools. You’ll need a compatible iPhone, an Android device with Wi-Fi Direct feature, an internet connection, and a PC or Mac. Once you have the necessary tools, make sure both devices are connected to the same wireless network.

Now, turn on Wi-Fi Direct on both phones. On the Android device, go to the ‘Wi-Fi’ settings and look for the ‘Wi-Fi Direct’ option. On the iPhone, open the ‘Settings’ app, and go to the ‘Wi-Fi’ page. Tap on the Wi-Fi Direct option to enable it.

Next, open the ‘Files’ app on your Android device. Here, you’ll find a list of available Wi-Fi Direct devices. Look for your iPhone in the list and tap on it to connect. Once your iPhone is connected, you’ll be able to view all the contents of your iPhone from your Android device.

Now, it’s time to transfer the files. On the Android device, select the files you want to transfer from your iPhone, then tap the ‘Send’ button. The files will then be transferred wirelessly to your Android device.

Finally, you can view the files you’ve transferred from your iPhone to your Android device. You’ll be able to find the transferred files in the ‘Files’ app on your Android device. There, you can view, edit, delete, or share them with ease.

With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to transfer files from your iPhone to your Android phone wirelessly without any hassle. So go ahead, give it a try and enjoy the convenience of transferring files between devices.
