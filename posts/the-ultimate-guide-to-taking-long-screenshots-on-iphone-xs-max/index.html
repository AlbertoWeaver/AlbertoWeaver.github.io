1. Understanding iPhone XS Max Long Screenshot#

Taking screenshots on iPhone XS Max is one of the best ways to capture and share moments and ideas from your device. But have you ever wanted to capture more than just a quick snapshot of something on your phone? Long screenshots allow you to take a scrolling screenshot of everything within a single view. Here’s how to take long screenshots on an iPhone XS Max.

First, open the web page or chat you want to take a screenshot of and then press and hold the power button and the volume up button at the same time. This will take a standard screenshot as usual. Once the screenshot is taken, a preview of it will appear at the bottom left of your screen. Tap on it and a new menu will appear.

In the top right corner of the new menu you will see the ‘Full Page’ option. Tap on it and your long screenshot will be taken. The iPhone XS Max will then take a few seconds to generate the long screenshot and save it to your photos. You can then share it with your friends or save it for later use.

Lastly, if you want to take multiple long screenshots, you can press the ‘Full Page’ button again and iPhone XS Max will take another long screenshot of the page. This way, you can capture as much information as you need without having to take multiple standard screenshots.

There you have it! Taking long screenshots on an iPhone XS Max is easy and can be done in a few simple steps. Now you can capture those important memories or conversations and share them with your friends.

3. Editing Long Screenshot in iPhone XS Max#

The iPhone XS Max has a great feature that not many know about, called Long Screenshot. Long Screenshot allows users to take longer screenshots of a certain webpage or article without having to scroll down. It is especially useful when trying to capture a lot of information on a single page. Here’s how to use Long Screenshot on an iPhone XS Max.

First, open up the webpage that you wish to capture. Once the page is open, press the side button and volume up button simultaneously and hold until the screenshot is taken. A thumbnail of the screenshot will appear on the bottom left of the screen.

Next, tap on the thumbnail to enter the editing screen. A preview of your screenshot should appear along with a few options. Tap on the Full Page option at the top of the screen to capture the entire page and not just the visible part.

After selecting the Full Page option, an expanded image of the screenshot will appear with a blue highlight outlining the content it will capture. To adjust the crop, simply drag the blue highlight to cover the area you need. Once the crop is finished, tap Done at the bottom of the screen.

Finally, a preview of the full screenshot will appear. Tap on Done at the top right of the screen to save the photo to your camera roll. And that’s how to take a Long Screenshot on your iPhone XS Max.
