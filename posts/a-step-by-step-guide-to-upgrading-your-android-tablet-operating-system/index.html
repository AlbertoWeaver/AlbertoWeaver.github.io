2. Installing the Updates#

Updating the operating system on your Android Tablet is a great way to get the most out of your device. It’s also a great way to ensure security and performance levels. Here’s how to update your tablet’s operating system:

First, open your tablet’s Settings Menu. Scroll down to the System section and select Check for Updates. This will check your tablet for any system updates. If an update is available, select Downloading and then Install this Update.

Once the update is installed, the system will restart to complete the installation. During this time, you might see a few messages asking you to confirm the changes. Confirm the changes, and then your device will start up again with the new operating system update.

Some apps on your tablet may require updates to work properly after the system update. To update apps, open the Google Play Store and select My Apps. Apps with available updates will show up in the Updates tab. Select Update All to automatically download and install the necessary updates.

Finally, to make sure you’re running the most up-to-date version of the operating system, periodically check the System section in the Settings Menu for available updates. If an update is available, select it and follow the same steps as before. Doing this will ensure that your tablet is running the latest version of the operating system.
